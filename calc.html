Calc 2023 Problem 1 (5pts)

Consider \(z=z(x,y)\) defined implicitly via \(F(\frac{z}{x},\frac{y}{x})=0\). Show that \begin{align*} x\frac{\partial z}{\partial x} + y\frac{\partial z}{\partial y} &= z \end{align*}

Step 1: Notice the \(\frac{\partial z}{\partial x}\) and \(\frac{\partial z}{\partial y}\) in the problem, we want to take derivatives of \(F(\frac{z}{x},\frac{y}{x})\) to obtain relations on those terms.
\begin{align} \frac{\partial F}{\partial x} &= \frac{\partial F}{\partial u}\frac{\partial u}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial F}{\partial v}\frac{\partial v}{\partial x} \\&= \frac{\partial F}{\partial u}\left(\frac{\partial z}{\partial x}\cdot\frac{1}{x}-z\cdot\frac{1}{x^2}\right) + \frac{\partial F}{\partial v}\left(-\frac{y}{x^2}\right) \\\frac{\partial F}{\partial y} &= \frac{\partial F}{\partial u}\frac{\partial u}{\partial y} + \frac{\partial F}{\partial v}\frac{\partial v}{\partial y} \\&= \frac{\partial F}{\partial u}\left(\frac{\partial z}{\partial y}\cdot\frac{1}{x}\right) + \frac{\partial F}{\partial v}\left(\frac{1}{x}\right) \end{align}

(...)

Step 2: Since \(F(\frac{z}{x},\frac{y}{x})\) is a constant on \(z=z(x,y)\), its derivatives are zero. Thus we obtain:
\begin{align} \frac{\partial F}{\partial u}\left(\frac{\partial z}{\partial x}\cdot\frac{1}{x}-z\cdot\frac{1}{x^2}\right) + \frac{\partial F}{\partial v}\left(-\frac{y}{x^2}\right) &= 0 \end{align} \begin{align} \frac{\partial F}{\partial u}\left(\frac{\partial z}{\partial y}\cdot\frac{1}{x}\right) + \frac{\partial F}{\partial v}\left(\frac{1}{x}\right) &= 0 \end{align}

(...)

Step 3: We don't know what are \(\frac{\partial F}{\partial u}\) and \(\frac{\partial F}{\partial v}\). Let's try eliminate them.

The easier one to eliminate would be \(\frac{\partial F}{\partial v}\). After elimination, we get: \begin{align} \frac{\partial F}{\partial u}\cdot\left(\frac{\partial z}{\partial x}\cdot\frac{1}{x}-z\cdot\frac{1}{x^2}+\frac{\partial z}{\partial y}\cdot\frac{y}{x^2}\right) &= 0 \end{align} The second factor is what we are looking for.

Calc 2023 Problem 7 (20pts)

(a) Show that \begin{align*} \nabla\cdot(f\nabla g - g\nabla f) = f\nabla^2 g - g\nabla^2 f \end{align*} and use it to show that \begin{align*} \unicode{x222F} \left(f\frac{\partial g}{\partial n}-g\frac{\partial f}{\partial n}\right) dS - \iiint \left(f\nabla^2 g - g\nabla^2 f\right) dV \end{align*} where \(S\) is the surface of the volume \(V\) and \(n\) is the outward normal on the surface \(S\).
(b) Use this result to show that for \(r = \sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}\) \begin{align*} \unicode{x222F} \left(\frac{1}{r}\nabla g-g\nabla(\frac{1}{r})\right)\cdot ndS = \iiint_V \frac{1}{r}\nabla^2 g dV + k \end{align*} where \(k = 4\pi g(0,0,0)\) if \(V\) encloses the origin and \(k=0\) otherwise.
